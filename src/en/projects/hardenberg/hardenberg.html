BERLIN-CHARLOTTENBURG

House Hardenberg



Glass Floor Area: 10.900 square meters
Investitionsvolumen: € 22 Million
Types of Use: Office, Retail
Architect: Paul Schwebes
Year of Construction: 1956
Size of the Project Team: 3 Employees

In 2002, we had the opportunity to give this architecturally outstanding, but heavily neglected 1950s building a new lease of life. It was necessary to strengthen the qualities of Haus Hardenberg and combine them with our goals for a contemporary and sustainable use.

At the beginning of the project, we defined the not inconsiderable changes in appearance required for use as a modern office and commercial building. All interventions should act as if they had existed since the original construction of the building, so that today they can hardly be distinguished from the original building fabric by experts.

For the faithful and sensitive restoration of House Hardenberg, Hühne Development Services has been awarded the Federal Prize for Craft in the Preservation of Monuments.
