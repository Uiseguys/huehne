BERLIN

Historic Apartments



Gross Floor Are: 5.600 square meter
Type of Use: Apartments
Year of Construction: 1909

The aim of the revitalization was to restore the historic character of the building. The building was rarely realized with great attention to detail even then. For example, tiles, window and door fittings were specially designed for the house and manufactured as a special order. Over the next century, however, many of these details were lost.

In the course of the revitalization, for example, historical door fittings were reproduced, the original wall colors in the stairwells were determined by a monument expert and historical tiles were faithfully reproduced. The bathrooms were redesigned on the basis of a comprehensive analysis of contemporary bathrooms in Berlin and other major European cities such as London and Paris.

The extraordinary and prestigious residential complex - the building complex is arranged around a park-like courtyard - was designed by the Norwegian landscape painter Adelsteen Normann, who lived and worked in Berlin at the beginning of the 20th century. He had commissioned the Berlin architect Hermann Bentzen with the planning.
