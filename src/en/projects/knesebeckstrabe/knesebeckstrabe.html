BERLIN-CHARLOTTENBURG

Knesebeckstraße 3

Gross Floor Area: 3.000 square meters
Types of Use: Offices, Retail
Year of Construction: 1965
Revitalization: 2008 - 2009
Design Concept Revitalization: Matthias C. Hühne

The original appearance of this office building, which was built in 1965 in the prime location of the Berlin City West, was a negative example of architecture of the 1960s. A retention of the existing design concept was not considered.

There was a complete redesign of the building according to the design concept of Matthias C. Hühne. The new architecture is based on successful models of classical modernism. A consistent design philosophy shapes the building both outside and inside. Authorization and implementation plans were drawn up by an experienced planning office. Today, the building has almost the same quality as a new building.

The result corresponds to our basic approach, in which the tenant is the focal point during the planning phase. The building was fully let within just six months of completion.
