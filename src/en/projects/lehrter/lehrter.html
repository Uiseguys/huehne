BERLIN-CENTER

Lehrter City District



Gross Floor Area: 145.000 square meters
Types of Use: Offices, Retail
Masterplan Architect: Oswald Mathias Ungers
Supervision by Hühne Development Services GmbH: 1997 bis 2003
Size of the Project Team: 4 Employees

Between 1997 and 2003, Hühne Development Services was responsible for a substantial part of this important inner-city area. Our goal was to create the best possible conditions for attractive development and successful marketing.

The Lehrter City District would have the potential to compete with the best locations in Berlin, such as Potsdamer Platz. Its location directly on the banks of the Spree vis à vis the Chancellery as well as the unique transport infrastructure offer first-class conditions for this.

The focus was on the optimization of the urban planning concept as well as conceptional building planning. For example, the location of office entrances, shops and restaurants, underground car park access and delivery from a cross-project perspective should be defined.

Representative office buildings were planned for the properties we managed (red). Hotels, apartments and cultural facilities, however, we saw mainly on the attractive areas around the Humboldthafen.
